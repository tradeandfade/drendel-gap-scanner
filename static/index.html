Create a Free Alpaca Account

Go to alpaca.markets and sign up for a free account. You can use a Paper Trading account (no real money needed). Paper accounts give you the same market data access as live accounts.
2

Generate API Keys

Once logged in to Alpaca, go to your Paper Trading dashboard. Click "Generate API Keys" (or "Regenerate" if you've done this before). You'll see two values: API Key ID and Secret Key. Copy both — you'll paste them below.

Important: The Secret Key is only shown once. Save it somewhere safe.
3

Enter Your Keys Below

Paste your Alpaca API keys here. They are stored locally on this server only — never sent anywhere else.
4

Add Your Watchlist

After your keys are validated, go to the Settings tab and add your stock symbols to the watchlist. Enter them one per line or comma-separated (e.g., AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, TSLA). The scanner will start loading historical data and scanning for gap zones.
5

Adjust Your Settings

In the Settings tab, configure:

Alert Sensitivity: Set to 0% to only alert when price is actually in a gap zone, or increase to get early proximity warnings.

Refresh Interval: How often the scanner checks prices (default: 5 minutes).

Lookback Period: How far back to scan for gaps (default: 252 days = 1 trading year).
6

Understanding the Scanner

The Scanner tab shows three sections:

Support Gap Alerts (Green): Stocks where price is pulling back into a gap-up zone. These zones act as potential support — price may bounce from here.

Resistance Gap Alerts (Red): Stocks where price is pushing up into a gap-down zone. These zones act as potential resistance — price may reverse here.

Untested Gap Alerts (Blue): Newly formed gaps that haven't been tested yet. On first contact, they convert to either support or resistance.

Each alert card shows the zone boundaries, how deep price is into the zone, and whether this is the zone's first test.
